Accessibility

CoCar is committed to inclusivity, following Australian accessibility guidelines (NDIS, ADN, City of Melbourne). We provide:

  • Fully accessible app (responsive, screen reader compatible, keyboard-navigable)
  • Call and chatbot support
  • Accessible vehicles through NDIS partnership
  • Dedicated drivers for mobility-impaired students
  • Free disabled parking spots for CoCar drivers
  • Low-sensory, noise-free ride options

Personas considered include students with low vision, blindness, cerebral palsy, and Deaf students.

Methods

We followed a user-centred, iterative design process, focusing on empathy, research, prototyping, and co-design with students. Goals included:

  • Enhancing campus life and social connections
  • Encouraging sustainable transport
  • Reducing financial burdens
  • Promoting accessibility

Research & Problem Formulation

Insights were drawn from background research, personas, and secondary sources. Key “How Might We” questions guided our design to solve issues like accessibility, cost, social isolation, and efficiency.

Idea Generation & Sketching

Using the 10+10 sketching method, we generated a wide range of ideas, narrowing down to 10 that addressed accessibility and user needs. Feedback focused on incentives, safety, and practicality.

Findings

Key insights included:

  • Drivers require incentives: financial, parking access, and social recognition
  • Students benefit from community forums and social matching
  • Safety depends on verified drivers and visible profiles
  • Syncing with university timetables improves ease-of-use
  • Low fares and environmental/social benefits encourage adoption over Uber

Policies & Safety

  • Punctuality and communication expectations
  • Vehicle cleanliness and hygiene
  • Accessibility-sensitive environment (e.g., music, sensory considerations)
  • Ride reimbursement system (auto-debit like Uber)

What Next?

  • Improve AI chat clarity and discoverability.
  • Test with a larger student pool for scalability.
  • Explore integration with university transport systems for enhanced adoption.

Reflection

Working on CoCar highlighted the impact of iterative UX design in creating real-world solutions. The project deepened my skills in user research, prototyping, and service design, resulting in a product that combines usability, social value, and sustainability.
